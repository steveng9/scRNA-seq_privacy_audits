  The justification chain: Silverman (1986, §4) establishes that KDE in $d$ dimensions converges at rate $n^{-2/(d+4)}$, so above ~10k samples in PCA-150 the marginal MISE improvement from additional samples is    
  negligible relative to PCA truncation error. Politis–Romano–Wolf (1999) provide the formal theory for inference based on subsamples. The DOMIAS reference implementation that we're already using                   
  (domias.baselines_optimized) operates on subsampled inputs in their own experiments.

  Important distinction: for the distance-based baselines (MC, GAN_leaks, GAN_leaks_cal), batching is not subsampling — batch_d_min computes the exact per-row minimum distance one chunk at a time, mathematically   
  identical to the unbatched version. So those baselines remain identical to the original Hilprecht (2019) and Chen et al. (2020) formulations; we only avoid materializing the full $n_\text{test} \times
  n_\text{synth}$ distance matrix. This is the same trick sc_baseline.py:batch_GAN_leaks already uses for gan_leaks_sc.                                                                                               
                                                
  Only the KDE step is genuinely lossy (subsamples synth/ref to ≤20k). I'll document both behaviors clearly in the script.
